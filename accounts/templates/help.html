About The Product

NYC Accessible Streets is an application designed to provide NYC street accessibility data to individuals with mobility issues or visual impairments. Millions of disabled individuals or elderly gets stranded on the streets due to being dropped off at an inaccessible location by their ride-share services. Our product tackles that problem by allowing users to search for any address in new york city and view nearby accessible ramps and signal poles, as well as inaccessible raised sidewalks. They can also view reports of temporarily inaccessible infrastructures near their searched locations. This will ensure that these users get to their destination safely.

Additionally, registered user can interact with the community by submitting reports, adding updates to reports, and verify the status of reports by reacting with a thumbs up button. Logged-in users also have the ability to add their frequently visited addresses to a list of favorites and share their favorite locations with friends and family.


FAQ

I see inaccessible ramp/signal pole on the street. How can I report it?

On the left-hand side of the main page click Report Issue on the card whose location you want to report. Then fill out the issue and description options and click Submit Report to notify us. After the report is submitted, you should see the pin icon change, and the card should now have be outlined in red and show the option for all users to to View Issue. You can add updates to the issue, thumbs up to verify the issue, or mark it as resovled when the location becomes accessible again.

Can I share addresses with my family?

Of course! You can easily copy addresses of accessible infrastructures by clicking on any card on the left side of the map page. If you want to share all pins near an address, simply click on the share icon on the right side right above the map to share that entire address.

How can I access low vision mode?

If our map is not accessible for you, you can alternatively view information of all the infrastructures using our low vision view. On the map page, click on the Low Vision View button on the navigation bar. You will now see an alternative tabular view of all the pin information. To go back to map view, simply click on the Map View button on the navigation bar.

How do I save frequently visited locations?

On the right hand side of the map page, you can click the 'add address to favorite' button to add the location to your favorites. Once the it has been successfully added, you should see 'address favorited!' on the screen. You can then view your list of favorites by clicking on the My Favorites button in the navigation bar. Only registered users have this ability to create their own list, so be sure to create an account and log in first.

Is information on this website moderated?

We have admin and staff members moderating all reports and users during working hours, Monday-Friday. If we see any inappropriate comments or spam, they will be immediately removed. Any false information will also be removed from the website as soon as possible. Users that consistently submit inappropriate content will be warned and removed.

This website is not compatible with my screen reader, what can I do?

If you notice any part of the website that is not screen-reader compatible, please contact us using the form below by specifying the model of your assitive technology and the issue you're experiencing. We will do our best to help.
